# Vision

A personal assistant that knows you deeply, meets you where you are, and helps you live the life you actually want.

---

## The Idea

Most productivity tools ask you to conform to them. You learn the app, maintain the system, feed the machine. This system inverts that relationship. It conforms to you. It listens, learns, and builds a living model of who you are — your habits, your people, your places, your goals, your energy patterns, your quirks. Then it uses that understanding to help, quietly and naturally.

There is no "app to open." You talk to Claude. On your terminal, in your IDE, through a scheduled trigger — wherever you already are. The system is always there, always aware of your context, always ready to help without being asked.

---

## What It Knows

**Your identity** — name, timezone, location, biography. The basics that let the system address you properly and understand your context.

**Your people** — everyone in your life. Names, aliases (including Hebrew), relationships, what they're up to. When someone texts you saying they're stuck in traffic, the system quietly notes that. When you later ask "what's going on with Mom?", it knows.

**Your places** — home, office, gym, favorite restaurant. With coordinates, so when your phone reports your GPS, the system knows you're at the office and can filter your tasks accordingly.

**Your facts** — preferences, habits, health info, opinions — anything that makes you, you. Each fact carries provenance: did you state it, did the system infer it, or was it extracted from a conversation?

**Your goals and values** — the full GTD horizons of focus, from daily tasks through life purpose. This hierarchy gives the system the ability to help you prioritize: not just "what's next?" but "what actually matters?"

**What it doesn't know yet** — the system actively tracks its own knowledge gaps and weaves clarification questions naturally into future conversations.

---

## How It Learns

**Through conversation** — when you mention personal information, the system stores it. Explicitly stated facts carry the highest confidence.

**Through observation** — your phone pushes GPS locations, IM notification captures, and calendar events. The system processes these signals to know where you are, what people in your life are doing, and what's on your schedule.

**Through direct input** — "Remember that I'm vegetarian." Done.

---

## How It Helps

**Task management without the management** — you say "I need to call the dentist." The system creates an action, tags it `@phone`, sets energy to `low`. You never opened a todo app.

**Project awareness** — "I'm planning an office move." The system tracks actions against it. It notices when a project has no next action and gently prompts.

**Inbox as safety net** — not everything is immediately actionable. Ideas, mentions, half-formed plans go to the inbox. The system helps you process them later.

**Proactive, not reactive** — overdue actions get a gentle mention. Stale projects are flagged. Morning interactions include today's schedule. But it's never pushy.

---

## What Success Looks Like

You wake up. A morning summary arrives: "Good morning. You have a 10am with the team, 3 actions due today, and you're still waiting on the plumber. Your mom mentioned she's feeling better."

You reply: "Thanks. Add 'buy flowers for mom' to my list."

Done. No app opened. No form filled. The system knows it's a shopping item, tags it `@errands`, and it'll show up next time you're near a store.

Not a tool you use, but a presence that helps.
